# Pneŋ Examples (VOS v0.6)

[Overview](README.md) | [Orthography](pneng-orthography.md) | [Grammar](pneng-grammar.md) | [Lexicon](pneng-lexicon.md) | [Examples](pneng-examples.md) | [Sample Text](pneng-sample-text.md)

All examples enforce:

1. VOS core order.
2. Adverb block immediately after verb group and before `ta`.
3. `ta` object marker.
4. Prefix conjunctions: `CONJ clause-A clause-B`.
5. `ðæt`-first relatives with resumptive pronoun.
6. No trailing PPs after the subject unless they are unambiguous CoreComps of a motion or stative-location verb.

English glosses use standard punctuation.

---

## 1. Basic Clauses

### 1.1 Intransitive

| Pneŋ | Gloss |
|---|---|
| `run fast hī` | He runs fast. |
| `wok slowli šī` | She walks slowly. |
| `ərajv lejt wī` | We arrive late. |
| `wil gō sūn wī tu ðe siti` | We will go to the city soon. |

`tu ðe siti` is a directional CoreComp of `gō` following the subject.

### 1.2 Transitive

| Pneŋ | Gloss |
|---|---|
| `sī klīrli ta æ kar šī` | She clearly sees a car. |
| `di sī jesterdej ta æ kar šī` | She saw a car yesterday. |
| `rid slowli ta ðe bük aj` | I read the book slowly. |
| `lajk veri mätš ta ðe stor hī` | He likes the store very much. |

### 1.3 Copula

| Pneŋ | Gloss |
|---|---|
| `bī veri tajərd šī` | She is very tired. |
| `bī æ tīčər hī` | He is a teacher. |
| `bī in ðe gardən ðej` | They are in the garden. |
| `di bī juŋ wī` | We were young. |

## 4. Prefix Coordination

| Pneŋ | Gloss |
|---|---|
| `ænd rid klīrli ta ðe bük aj rajt slowli ta æ nōt aj` | I read the book clearly and write a note slowly. |
| `bät gō sūn tu ðe park wī rimejn loŋ in hām wī` | We go soon to the park, but we remain long at home. |
| `or bī red it bī blū it` | It is red or it is blue. |
| `nor gō sūn tu ðe farm hī rimejn loŋ in hām hī` | He neither goes soon to the farm nor remains long at home. |
| `sō di rejn hard it rimejn insajd wī` | It rained hard, so we remained inside. |

For `bät` and `nor` examples: `tu ðe park` and `tu ðe farm` are directional CoreComps of `gō`, placed after the subject; `in hām` is a locative CoreComp of `rimejn`, also after the subject. Both verbs are stative-location or motion verbs that license post-subject PPs.

---

## 5. Prefix Subordination

| Pneŋ | Gloss |
|---|---|
| `if rejn hard it rimejn in hām wī` | If it rains hard, we remain at home. |
| `bikoz rejn hard it rimejn hīr wī` | Because it rains hard, we remain here. |
| `oldou di bī veri tajərd aj wörk hard aj` | Although I was very tired, I worked hard. |
| `wen ərajv ontajm šī start nau ta ðe mītiŋ wī` | When she arrives on time, we start the meeting now. |
| `until finišt ta ðe tæsk hī not gō hī` | Until he finishes the task, he does not go. |

`in hām` and `hīr` in the first two examples are locative CoreComps of `rimejn`, following their respective subjects `wī`.

## 6. Questions

### 6.1 Polar

Polar questions use `ke` clause-initially.

| Pneŋ | Gloss |
|---|---|
| `ke lajk reeli ta jū šī` | Does she really like you? |
| `ke wil kəm tumoro ðej` | Will they come tomorrow? |

### 6.2 Content — Object

The wh-word replaces the object NP inside `ta`.

| Pneŋ | Gloss |
|---|---|
| `ke sī klīrli ta wat šī` | What does she clearly see? |
| `ke giv kindli ta wat hī tu šī` | What does he kindly give to her? |
| `ke lajk most ta wij hī` | Which one does he like most? |

### 6.3 Content — Subject

The wh-word replaces the subject NP.

| Pneŋ | Gloss |
|---|---|
| `ke sī klīrli ta ðe kar hū` | Who clearly sees the car? |
| `ke wörk hard in ðe farm hū` | Who works hard in the farm? |

`in ðe farm` is a locative adjunct in the adverb block before the subject wh-word `hū`.

### 6.4 Content — Oblique

The wh-word occupies the oblique slot at the end of the clause.

| Pneŋ | Gloss |
|---|---|
| `ke gō sūn hī tu wer` | Where does he go soon? |
| `ke ərajv lejt šī wen` | When does she arrive late? |
| `ke brek ta ðe glas hū waj` | Why does who break the glass? |

The last example is a double wh-question (subject and reason). In practice single wh-questions are far more common.

## 7. Imperative and Prohibitive

| Pneŋ | Gloss |
|---|---|
| `du rid klīrli ta ðis bük jū` | Read this book clearly. |
| `du opən softli ta ðe windou jū` | Open the window softly. |
| `du wejt kalmli nīr ðe brij jū` | Wait calmly near the bridge. |
| `not du brek sudənli ta ðe glas jū` | Do not suddenly break the glass. |
| `not du gō lejt jū tu ðe markit` | Do not go late to the market. |

`nīr ðe brij` is in the adverb block before the subject `jū`.
`tu ðe markit` is a directional CoreComp of `gō`, following the subject `jū`.

---

## 8. Hortative and Optative

| Pneŋ | Gloss |
|---|---|
| `let bild nau nīr ðe fīld ta æ nū brij wī` | Let us build a new bridge near the field now. |
| `let siŋ tugaðər wī` | Let us sing together. |
| `hop rejn sūn it` | May it rain soon. |
| `hop rimejn sejf wī` | May we remain safe. |

`nīr ðe fīld` is in the adverb block before `ta`, unambiguously a locative of `bild`.
`tugaðər` is in the adverb block before the subject `wī`.

---

## 9. Relative Clauses (`ðæt`-first, resumptive)

| Pneŋ | Gloss |
|---|---|
| `ðæt lajk reeli ta it šī æ dogg` | a dog that she really likes |
| `ðæt di rid jesterdej ta it aj ðe bük` | the book that I read yesterday |
| `ðæt sī klīrli in ðe markit ta hī wī ðe man` | the man that we clearly see in the market |
| `ðæt wörk hard in ðe farm šī ðe wuman` | the woman who works hard in the farm |

The resumptive pronoun (`it` for non-human, `hī`/`šī`/`ðej` for human) fills the gap position inside the relative clause.

For the third example: `in ðe markit` is in the adverb block of `sī` inside the relative, before `ta`, so it unambiguously locates the act of seeing rather than either the subject `wī` or the object `hī`.

---

## 10. Content Clauses

| Pneŋ | Gloss |
|---|---|
| `nō ðæt sī sūn ta ðe rōd hī ðej` | They know that he soon sees the road. |
| `belīv ðæt bī trū it šī` | She believes that it is true. |
| `tel frankli ðæt di wörk hard šī aj tu hī` | I frankly tell him that she worked hard. |

Content clause objects of T and D verbs are introduced by `ðæt` and are not marked with `ta`.

## 11. Verb Group Examples (Tense, Aspect, Negation)

| Pneŋ | Gloss |
|---|---|
| `di sī jesterdej ta ðe kar šī` | She saw the car yesterday. |
| `wil sī tumoro ta ðe kar šī` | She will see the car tomorrow. |
| `iŋ sī nau ta ðe kar šī` | She is seeing the car now. |
| `dän sī alredi ta ðe kar šī` | She has already seen the car. |
| `jus sī ta ðe kar šī` | She just saw the car. |
| `not sī nevr ta ðe kar šī` | She never sees the car. |
| `di not sī ta ðe kar šī` | She did not see the car. |
| `wil not gō tu ðe siti hī` | He will not go to the city. |

`tu ðe siti` is a directional CoreComp of `gō`, following subject `hī`.
