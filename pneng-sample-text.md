A short narrative demonstrating VOS order, prefix conjunctions, `ðæt`-relatives, and the verb group system.
Each line is given with an interlinear parse and an English gloss.

---

## Scene: A Morning in the Village

### Line 1
```
wejk örlī šī
```
*wake  early  she*
She wakes early.

---

### Line 2
```
sī klīrli bifor ðe markit ta ðe sun šī
```
*see  clearly  before  the  market  OBJ  the  sun  she*
She clearly sees the sun before the market.

The temporal PP `bifor ðe markit` is in the adverb block before `ta`, unambiguously modifying the act of seeing rather than the subject or object.

---

### Line 3
```
ænd opən softli ta ðe dōr šī gō sūn tu ðe gardən šī
```
*and  [open softly OBJ the door she]  [go soon to the garden she]*
She softly opens the door and goes soon to the garden.

`tu ðe gardən` is a directional CoreComp of `gō`, following the subject `šī`.

---

### Line 4
```
bī kōld nau ðe windou
```
*be  cold  now  the  window*
The window is cold now.

---

### Line 5
```
sī klīrli ta ðe ðæt wörk hard in ðe fīld hī ðe farmər šī
```
*see  clearly  OBJ  [REL work hard in the field HE the farmer]  she*
She clearly sees the farmer who works hard in the field.

The relative clause `ðæt wörk hard in ðe fīld hī` has `in ðe fīld` as a CoreComp of `wörk` inside the relative.
The head noun `ðe farmər` closes the relative NP.
The matrix subject is `šī`.

---

### Line 6
```
wejt kalmli nīr ðe brij hī
```
*wait  calmly  near  the  bridge  he*
He waits calmly near the bridge.

`nīr ðe brij` is in the adverb block before the subject `hī`, unambiguously a locative adjunct of `wejt`.

---

### Line 7
```
giv kindli ta æ bæg hī tu šī
```
*give  kindly  OBJ  a  bag  he  to  her*
He kindly gives a bag to her.

---

### Line 8
```
æsk softli ta æ fejvər šī tu hī
```
*ask  softly  OBJ  a  favor  she  to  him*
She softly asks a favor of him.

---

### Line 9
```
bikoz di rejn hard it di rimejn insajd wī
```
*because  [PAST rain hard it]  [PAST remain inside we]*
Because it rained hard, we remained inside.

---

### Line 10
```
rid kalmli ta ðe ðæt di rajt lejt ta it šī ðe letər aj
```
*read  calmly  OBJ  [REL PAST write late OBJ IT she  the letter]  I*
I calmly read the letter that she wrote late.

The relative clause is `ðæt di rajt lejt ta it šī`.
`rajt` is transitive, so the resumptive object is `ta it`.
`šī` is the subject of the relative clause.
`ðe letər` is the head noun.

---

### Line 11
```
nō ðæt di kəm örlī šī ðe man
```
*know  COMP  [PAST come early she]  the man*
The man knows that she came early.

Content clause complements of T verbs are introduced by `ðæt` and are not marked with `ta`.

---

### Line 12
```
bät wil gō tu ðe siti ðej rimejn hīr wī
```
*but  [will go to the city they]  [remain here we]*
They will go to the city, but we remain here.

`tu ðe siti` is a directional CoreComp of `gō`, following the subject `ðej`.

---

### Line 13
```
wen finišt ta ðe tæsk ðej start nau ta æ nū plæn wī
```
*when  [finish OBJ the task they]  [start now OBJ a new plan we]*
When they finish the task, we start a new plan now.

---

### Line 14
```
not du gō lejt tu ðe markit jū
```
*NEG  IMP  go  late  to  the  market  you*
Do not go late to the market.

`tu ðe markit` is a directional CoreComp of `gō`, following the subject `jū`.

---

### Line 15
```
let bild nau nīr ðe fīld ta æ sejf hām wī
```
*HORT  build  now  near  the  field  OBJ  a  safe  home  we*
Let us build a safe home near the field now.

`nīr ðe fīld` is in the adverb block before `ta`, unambiguously a locative adjunct of `bild` rather than a modifier of the subject or object.

---

### Line 16
```
hop rimejn worm duriŋ ðe nait wī
```
*OPT  remain  warm  during  the  night  we*
May we remain warm during the night.

`duriŋ ðe nait` is in the adverb block before the subject `wī`.

---

### Line 17
```
sī sudənli in ðe forest ta ðe fajər šī
```
*see  suddenly  in  the  forest  OBJ  the  fire  she*
She suddenly sees the fire in the forest.

`in ðe forest` is in the adverb block before `ta`, unambiguously a scene-setting locative of the act of seeing rather than a modifier of either `šī` or `ðe fajər`.
To specify the fire as being in the forest rather than the scene, use a relative on the object: `ta ðe ðæt börn in ðe forest it fajər`.

---

### Line 18
```
ænd kraj loudli šī run fast tu ðe vilij šī
```
*and  [cry loudly she]  [run fast to the village she]*
She cries loudly and runs fast to the village.

`tu ðe vilij` is a directional CoreComp of `run`, following the subject `šī`.

---

### Line 19
```
hīr klīrli ta ðe kraj fram ðe vilij ðe s farmər
```
*hear  clearly  OBJ  the cry from the village  the-PL farmers*
The farmers clearly hear the cry from the village.

`fram ðe vilij` is a post-nominal PP inside the object NP `ðe kraj`, specifying the origin of the cry.
Post-nominal PPs are licensed inside NBar: `(Det)? (Num)? (AdjP*) N (PP*)`.

---

### Line 20
```
ænd gō kalmli tu ðe forest ðe s farmər kæri kareföli ta ðe s tūl ðej
```
*and  [go calmly to the forest the-PL farmers]  [carry carefully OBJ the-PL tools they]*
The farmers go calmly to the forest and they carefully carry the tools.

`tu ðe forest` is a directional CoreComp of `gō`, following the subject.

---

### Line 21
```
bät börn still ðe fajər rimejn sejf ðe s trī
```
*but  [burn still the fire]  [remain safe the-PL trees]*
The fire still burns but the trees remain safe.

`börn` is used intransitively.
`sejf` is a predicate adjective following `rimejn`.

---

### Line 22
```
fajnali stop sudənli ðe fajər wen fol hard ðe rejn
```
*finally  stop  suddenly  the  fire  when  [fall hard the rain]*
The fire finally stops suddenly when the rain falls hard.

`fajnali` and `sudənli` are both in the adverb block of `stop`.
Multiple PostVAdvs are permitted per the grammar.

---

### Line 23
```
ænd sit tugaðər in ðe markit ðe s pörzən tel frankli ta ðæt di hapən it ðej tu ðe s neibər
```
*and  [sit together in the market the-PL people]  [tell frankly OBJ COMP what-happened they to the-PL neighbors]*
The people sit together in the market, and they frankly tell the neighbors what happened.

`tugaðər` and `in ðe markit` are both in the adverb block of `sit`, before the subject `ðe s pörzən`.
The object of `tel` is a `ðæt`-clause: `ðæt di hapən it` where `it` is a dummy subject of the embedded intransitive `hapən`.
The recipient `tu ðe s neibər` follows as a CoreComp after the subject `ðej`.

---

### Line 24
```
nō ðæt dän rimejn sejf wī ðej
```
*know  COMP  [COMPL remain safe we]  they*
They know that we have remained safe.

---

### Line 25
```
hop ænd rimejn stroŋ wī bild tugaðər nau ta æ nū fūtjər wī
```
*OPT  and  [remain strong we]  [build together now OBJ a new future we]*
May we remain strong and build a new future together now.

`tugaðər` and `nau` are both in the adverb block of `bild`, before `ta`.

## Parse Reference

| Symbol | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `OBJ` | `ta` object marker |
| `COMP` | `ðæt` complementizer or relativizer |
| `REL` | relativizer use of `ðæt` (inside relative NP) |
| `PAST` | `di` past marker |
| `NEG` | `not` negation |
| `IMP` | `du` imperative particle |
| `HORT` | `let` hortative particle |
| `OPT` | `hop` optative particle |
| `COMPL` | `dän` completive aspect |
| `PROG` | `iŋ` progressive aspect |
| `PL` | `s` plural particle |
